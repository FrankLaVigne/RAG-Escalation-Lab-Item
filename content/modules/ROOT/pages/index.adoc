= RAG Escalation Lab
:navtitle: Welcome

Your customer needs a specialist model that knows their domain cold. The obvious move — "just fine-tune it" — is expensive, slow, and often unnecessary. This lab walks you through an *escalation ladder*: start with the cheapest intervention, measure, and only climb the next rung if the evidence demands it.

NOTE: The guiding principle of this workshop is **escalation of effort must be justified by evidence**.

== What you'll learn

Every rung of the ladder is a response to a real enterprise scenario — a customer with hundreds of documents, inconsistent structure, and a model that's never seen their domain. The lab begins with a raw baseline and progresses through retrieval, inference-time scaling, synthetic data generation, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning, with evaluation at every step.

* How to benchmark a stock LLM against a domain the model has never seen
* When retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is enough — and when it isn't
* How inference-time scaling (best-of-N) compares to training-time investment
* How to generate synthetic training data and parameter-efficiently fine-tune with QLoRA
* How to evaluate outcomes across all these approaches and choose the right rung for a real engagement

== The customer scenario

The customer you're walking into has a familiar problem: hundreds of documents — PDFs, Word files, PowerPoint decks, HTML pages — accumulated over years. Many are scanned or poorly structured. They contain domain-specific language, implicit rules, and institutional knowledge baked in by people who may no longer be around.

They don't come in asking for RAG or fine-tuning. They ask something simpler and harder: *"We have all this information. How do we actually use AI with it?"*

They've already experimented on their own. They uploaded a few documents into a chat interface, asked some questions, and got some useful answers — and enough wrong ones to lose trust. The pattern you'll hear: "It kind of works... but we can't rely on it. We can't scale this. We can't explain why it answers the way it does."

To make the scenario concrete and hands-on, the lab uses the *Basic Fantasy RPG* rulebook as the stand-in domain corpus. It's a real, freely available document with genuine structure, rules, edge cases, and terminology — the kind of thing a model trained on general web data handles poorly without grounding. Your job is to prove (or disprove, with evidence) whether fine-tuning is actually necessary to make the model useful on it, or whether a cheaper intervention gets the customer where they need to go.

The customer's definition of success is not perfect answers. It's answers that are grounded in their documents, consistent across queries, and defensible to their legal, compliance, or engineering team. That framing drives every decision the lab asks you to make.
